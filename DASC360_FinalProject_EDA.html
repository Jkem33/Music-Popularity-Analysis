# For all cases, cases being the names of the tracks, popularity is the response variable and all other variables (or columns) are predictors. I broke things down this way for two main reasons. First, we were asked to study exactly one response variable with this project. Second, popularity is the most appealing variable to understand (amongst the existing variables in this dataset), as understanding it could yield the secrets behind consistently making hit songs. 

# When it comes to the other predictors, most of them are musical measures pertaining to the individual tracks and have the potential to be good predictors. The only ones that are not musical measures are "Year" and "Duration". The variable "Year" is better suited as a predictor because it could indicate which musical era was most popular, and by correlation with musical measures, also inform us about the quirks of such a musical era. The variable "Duration" could tell us if length can drag a song down or perhaps boost it.
# Unnecessary or inadequate ones have already been trimmed, such as "Artist" and "Mode". The variable "Artist" refers to the author of the track and could help us filter the fame factor out of what makes a song popular, but would have been difficult to numerize for R. The variable "Mode" refers to the modality of the track, which was deemed minor enough to trim right from the start. 

# A variable that remained for now but could be trimmed is "Liveness" as it merely indicates whether the measure was recorded from a live session or not. Another take to this variable could be that the seeming presence of a live audience is in fact intentional and part of the track; Which is why this variable remains for now.

# In order to indicate the categorical variable "Genre", I used dummy variables "Pop" and "Metal". The number 1 under "Pop" means that the genre of the specific track (or row) is Pop; The number 0 means that the genre is not Pop. Similarly, the number 1 under "Metal" means that the genre of the specific track (or row) is Metal; The number 0 means that the genre is not Metal. If a track has "0" under both "Pop" and "Metal", it means that its genre is "Other".
